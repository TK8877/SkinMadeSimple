Skincare Basics

You need three things to start: a cleanser, a moisturiser, and SPF. Everything else is optional. Here's exactly why — and how to use them.

✦ The Essentials

Four steps. That's it.

No matter your skin type, age, or budget, every effective skincare routine is built on the same four foundational pillars. Master these consistently before adding anything else.

🧼

1. Cleanse

Removes dirt, oil, sunscreen, and pollution. Use a gentle, pH-balanced formula morning and evening. Avoid anything that makes skin feel "squeaky clean" — that signals over-stripping of natural oils.

🌸

2. Tone (Optional)

Restores pH balance after cleansing and primes skin to absorb serums and moisturisers more effectively. Hydrating toners are especially beneficial for dry or dull skin types.

💧

3. Moisturise

Every skin type needs hydration — including oily skin. A moisturiser seals in water, reinforces the skin barrier, and prevents oil overproduction caused by dehydration.

☀️

4. Protect (SPF)

The most effective anti-ageing product you can use. Apply SPF 30+ every morning — on cloudy days, indoors, year-round. UV damage is cumulative and permanent. This step is non-negotiable.

✦ Layering Order

Always thinnest to thickest.

The cardinal rule of skincare layering: apply products from the most watery consistency to the heaviest. This lets each product absorb properly instead of being blocked by a thicker layer above it.

  • 1st — Cleanser: Always the very first step.
  • 2nd — Toner / Essence: Lightest, almost water-like texture.
  • 3rd — Serum: Concentrated actives in a thin, fast-absorbing base.
  • 4th — Moisturiser: Locks in everything applied before it.
  • 5th — SPF (AM only): Final step every single morning — never under anything.

✦ Common Pitfalls

Mistakes to avoid from day one.

Most skincare frustrations come from a small number of easily avoidable habits. Knowing these upfront saves months of trial and error.

⚠️

Over-cleansing

Washing more than twice a day strips the skin's natural protective oils, triggering irritation, redness, and ironically — more oil production as your skin tries to compensate. Twice daily is the sweet spot.

🧪

Too Many Products at Once

Adding several new products simultaneously makes it impossible to identify what's helping — or causing a reaction. Introduce one product at a time and wait at least 1–2 weeks before adding the next.

🌞

Skipping SPF

UV exposure is responsible for up to 90% of visible skin ageing. No skincare routine is complete — or fully effective — without daily broad-spectrum SPF 30+ as the last morning step. No exceptions.

⏱️

Expecting Instant Results

Most active ingredients need 4–12 weeks of consistent use before visible improvements appear. Switching products every two weeks is the main reason people never see progress. Patience is the real secret.
