Skincare Basics

You don't need 12 products. You need the right 3–4 — done consistently. Here's exactly what they are and why they matter.

The Core Four

The essential steps every routine needs.

No matter your skin type, budget, or experience level, every effective skincare routine is built on the same four pillars. Master these before adding anything else.

🧼

1. Cleanse

Remove dirt, oil, sunscreen, and environmental pollutants. Use a gentle cleanser twice daily — morning and night. Avoid anything that leaves skin feeling "squeaky clean," which signals over-stripping.

🌸

2. Tone (optional)

Toners balance your skin's pH after cleansing and can remove residual cleanser. A good toner prepares your skin to absorb serums and moisturizers more effectively.

💧

3. Moisturize

Every skin type needs hydration — including oily skin. A moisturizer locks in water and reinforces the skin barrier. Without it, skin overproduces oil or becomes irritated and flaky.

☀️

4. SPF (AM only)

The single most effective anti-aging product you can use. Apply SPF 30 or higher every single morning — even indoors, even on cloudy days. UV damage is cumulative and irreversible.

Product Layering

Always go thinnest to thickest.

The golden rule of skincare layering is to apply products from the lightest (most watery) consistency to the heaviest (thickest). This ensures each product can penetrate the skin properly and isn't blocked by a heavier product above it.

For example: a water-based serum applied over a thick moisturizer can't absorb — the moisturizer creates a barrier. But when applied the correct way (serum first, moisturizer second), both work as intended.

Avoid These

The most common beginner mistakes.

Most skincare frustrations come from a handful of easily avoidable habits. Here's what to watch out for from day one.

⚠️

Over-cleansing

Washing more than twice a day strips the skin's natural protective oils, leading to irritation, redness, and paradoxically — more oil production as the skin tries to compensate.

🧪

Too Many Products at Once

Adding several new products simultaneously makes it impossible to identify what's working (or causing a reaction). Introduce one product at a time and wait 1–2 weeks before adding another.

😴

Skipping SPF

UV damage causes wrinkles, dark spots, and increases skin cancer risk. Sunscreen is the most proven anti-aging product in existence. No routine is complete without it in the morning.

⏱️

Expecting Instant Results

Most skincare products need 4–12 weeks of consistent use before visible improvements appear. Switching products too soon is one of the biggest reasons routines fail.
