Know What's In Your Products

Ingredients Decoded

Learn what key skincare ingredients do, who they're best for, and how to use them safely.

Hydrators & Humectants

Moisture-boosting ingredients.

All Skin Types

Hyaluronic Acid

A powerful humectant that draws water into the skin and holds up to 1,000x its weight in moisture. Best applied to damp skin followed by a moisturizer to seal it in.

Dry / Sensitive

Glycerin

One of the most effective humectants available. Found in most moisturizers, it pulls moisture from the air into your skin and is gentle enough for all skin types including sensitive skin.

Normal / Dry

Squalane

A lightweight, non-comedogenic oil that mimics the skin's natural sebum. Excellent at softening skin without clogging pores. Derived from olives or sugarcane.

Actives & Treatments

Targeted treatment ingredients.

Start Slow · Nighttime Only

Retinol (Vitamin A)

The gold standard anti-aging ingredient. Speeds up cell turnover, reduces fine lines, and improves texture. Start with 0.025–0.1% and use only at night. Can cause dryness and purging initially.

Brightening

Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)

A powerful antioxidant that brightens skin, fades dark spots, and boosts collagen. Best used in the morning. Look for formulas with 10–20% L-ascorbic acid and store away from light.

Exfoliant

Niacinamide (Vitamin B3)

A versatile ingredient that reduces redness, minimizes pores, controls oil, and strengthens the skin barrier. Works well with most other ingredients. Ideal for sensitive and oily skin types.

Exfoliant · AHA

Glycolic & Lactic Acid

Alpha-hydroxy acids that chemically exfoliate the top layer of skin, revealing smoother, brighter skin beneath. Lactic acid is gentler and better for beginners. Use 1–3x per week max.

Exfoliant · BHA · Oily Skin

Salicylic Acid

A beta-hydroxy acid that's oil-soluble, meaning it penetrates pores to dissolve congestion and reduce breakouts. Ideal for oily, acne-prone skin. Use 1–2x per week.

Barrier Repair

Ceramides

Naturally occurring lipids that make up about 50% of the skin barrier. Help repair and maintain the protective layer that keeps moisture in and irritants out. Excellent for dry, sensitive skin.

Ingredient Pairs to Avoid

What not to mix.

🚫

Retinol + AHAs/BHAs

Combining these can cause significant irritation and compromise the skin barrier. Use retinol at night and exfoliating acids on alternate nights.

🚫

Vitamin C + Niacinamide

Can reduce the effectiveness of Vitamin C. Apply Vitamin C first and wait 20–30 minutes before applying Niacinamide, or use them at different times of day.

🚫

Retinol + Vitamin C

Both can be irritating and their effectiveness can be reduced when used together. Use Vitamin C in the morning and Retinol at night.

✅

Hyaluronic Acid + Everything

HA works well with all other actives. It's a hydration booster that pairs beautifully with Vitamin C, Niacinamide, retinol, and SPF.
