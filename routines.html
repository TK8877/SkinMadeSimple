Your Personalized Plan

Daily Routines

Morning and evening routines tailored for every skin type. The secret? Simplicity and consistency — not more products.

Where to Start

Find your skin type first.

Before choosing a routine, it helps to understand which skin type you have. The easiest way: wash your face with a gentle cleanser, wait one hour without applying anything, and observe how your skin feels.

  • Oily: Shiny or greasy all over, especially T-zone
  • Dry: Tight, rough, or flaky patches; little shine
  • Combination: Oily T-zone (forehead, nose, chin) with normal or dry cheeks
  • Sensitive: Reacts easily to products — redness, stinging, or itching
  • Normal: Balanced, neither too oily nor too dry, minimal sensitivity

Step-by-Step Routines

Choose your skin type.

Each routine below includes recommended product types for both morning and evening. Remember: it's better to do a simple 3-step routine consistently than a 10-step routine occasionally.

🌅 Morning Routine

Focus on cleansing without over-stripping, and controlling shine through the day.

1

Gel or Foaming Cleanser

Removes overnight oil and sebum. Look for formulas with salicylic acid to gently unclog pores during cleansing.

2

Niacinamide Toner

Balances oil production and minimizes the appearance of enlarged pores. Apply with a cotton pad or pat directly into skin.

3

Oil-Free Gel Moisturizer

Oily skin still needs hydration. A lightweight, non-comedogenic gel formula hydrates without adding shine or clogging pores.

4

Matte Finish SPF 30+

A mattifying sunscreen controls shine throughout the day while protecting against UV damage. Never skip this step.

🌙 Evening Routine

Remove all makeup and sunscreen thoroughly, then treat congested pores.

1

Micellar Water or Balm Cleanser

First cleanse to break down makeup, sunscreen, and surface pollution without disturbing the skin barrier.

2

Foaming Cleanser (Double Cleanse)

Second cleanse ensures pores are fully clear of residue. Essential after wearing sunscreen or makeup.

3

BHA Exfoliant 1–2× /week

Salicylic acid penetrates oily pores and clears congestion. Use on alternating nights; do not combine with retinol on same night.

4

Light Gel Moisturizer

Even oily skin needs nighttime hydration to recover. A gel formula won't feel heavy or greasy as you sleep.

🌅 Morning Routine

Layer hydration and seal it in with richer formulas designed to nourish dry skin all day.

1

Creamy or Milky Cleanser

Gentle, non-stripping formulas that clean without disrupting the little natural moisture dry skin has. Avoid foaming cleansers.

2

Hydrating Toner or Essence

Add an extra layer of lightweight moisture before heavier products. Pat in gently — don't wipe.

3

Hyaluronic Acid Serum

Apply to still-damp skin to draw moisture in. Follow immediately with moisturizer to lock it in — critical step for dry skin.

4

Rich Cream Moisturizer

Look for ceramides, shea butter, or squalane. Thicker creams work best for dry skin to provide long-lasting hydration.

5

Hydrating SPF 50

Choose a dewy or moisturizing sunscreen formula for an added layer of comfort. Avoid matte formulas for dry skin.

🌙 Evening Routine

Focus on deep nourishment and overnight barrier repair.

1

Gentle Oil-Based Cleanser

Oil dissolves sunscreen and makeup while actually nourishing dry skin — unlike foaming cleansers which can strip it further.

2

Hydrating Toner

Layer in a glycerin or hyaluronic-acid-rich toner while skin is still slightly damp from cleansing.

3

Peptide or Ceramide Serum

Repair and rebuild the skin barrier as you sleep. Peptides signal the skin to produce more collagen; ceramides restore the protective lipid layer.

4

Rich Night Cream or Sleeping Mask

Seal everything in with a thick nourishing formula. Use a sleeping mask 1–2× per week for an extra intensive hydration treatment.

🌅 Morning Routine

Balance is the goal — hydrate dry zones without overloading the oily T-zone.

1

Balancing Gel Cleanser

Neither too stripping nor too nourishing — a balanced formula cleans all zones without aggravating either concern.

2

Niacinamide-Based Toner

Niacinamide simultaneously addresses oiliness (T-zone), redness, and texture — ideal for combination skin's multiple needs.

3

Light Lotion Moisturizer

A medium-weight moisturizer hydrates dry areas (cheeks) without over-loading oily zones. Avoid heavy creams or gel extremes.

4

Balanced SPF 30+

Choose a formula that's neither too mattifying nor too dewy — a fluid or lotion SPF typically works well.

🌙 Evening Routine

Customize by skin zone — richer on dry areas, lighter on oily zones.

1

Double Cleanse

Micellar water followed by a gel cleanser ensures all zones are thoroughly cleansed, especially if wearing SPF or makeup.

2

Niacinamide Serum (All Over)

Apply niacinamide all over for its balancing, pore-minimizing, and redness-reducing benefits. Works perfectly for combination skin.

3

Zone-Specific Moisturizing

Apply a lightweight gel on the T-zone and a richer cream on the cheeks. This "skin zone" approach gives each area exactly what it needs.

🌅 Morning Routine

Fewer ingredients, gentler formulas, and fragrance-free everything. Simplicity is the priority.

1

Fragrance-Free Cream Cleanser

Skip foaming cleansers — surfactants can trigger reactive skin. A fragrance-free cream or micellar water is far gentler.

2

Calming Toner or Essence

Look for centella asiatica, aloe vera, allantoin, or panthenol — all proven to soothe redness and calm inflammation.

3

Simple Ceramide Moisturizer

Fewer ingredients = less reaction risk. Ceramide-rich, fragrance-free moisturizers are the gold standard for sensitive skin.

4

Mineral Sunscreen SPF 30+

Zinc oxide-based "physical" sunscreens sit on top of skin rather than absorbing into it, making them significantly gentler for reactive skin than chemical filters.

🌙 Evening Routine

Keep it minimal. Let your skin's natural overnight repair process do the heavy lifting.

1

Micellar Water (No-rinse)

Often sufficient on its own for sensitive skin. Gently wipes away the day without any harsh rubbing or surfactant-heavy formulas.

2

Skin-Calming Serum

Centella asiatica, panthenol (Vitamin B5), and allantoin support skin healing and reduce overnight redness and irritation.

3

Fragrance-Free Night Moisturizer

Keep the ingredient list short. The fewer potential irritants, the better. Patch test any new moisturizer before applying to your full face.
