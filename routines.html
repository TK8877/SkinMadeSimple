Daily Routines

Morning and evening routines for every skin type. Consistency with a simple routine always beats an elaborate one done occasionally.

✦ Know Your Skin

Identify your skin type first.

The most effective routine is one tailored to your actual skin type. To identify yours: wash your face with a gentle cleanser, apply nothing, and observe how it feels after 60 minutes.

  • Oily: Visibly shiny or greasy, especially on the T-zone
  • Dry: Tight, rough, or flaky with minimal natural shine
  • Combination: Oily forehead, nose & chin with drier cheeks
  • Sensitive: Reacts easily with redness, stinging, or itching
  • Normal: Balanced, comfortable, minimal sensitivity or oiliness

Routine principles to remember

Keep these core ideas in mind as you build your personalised routine below:

  • Consistency beats complexity. A 3-step routine done daily outperforms a 10-step routine done occasionally.
  • Thinnest to thickest. Always apply watery products before heavier creams and oils.
  • SPF is the final morning step. Every single day — no exceptions.
  • Give products time. Most actives need 4–12 weeks before visible results appear.
  • One new product at a time. Introduce slowly so you can identify what works — and what doesn't.
✦ Step-by-Step

Choose your skin type.

Each routine below covers recommended product types for morning and evening. Start with the essentials — cleanser, moisturiser, SPF — and add steps gradually as your skin adjusts.

🌅 Morning

Control shine, hydrate without grease, protect all day.

1

Gel or Foaming Cleanser

Removes overnight sebum and bacteria. Look for salicylic acid formulas that gently decongest pores during the cleansing step itself.

2

Niacinamide Toner

Regulates oil production and visibly minimises pores. Apply with a cotton pad or pat directly into skin with clean fingertips.

3

Oil-Free Gel Moisturiser

Oily skin still needs hydration — skipping this triggers compensatory oil production. Choose non-comedogenic, lightweight formulas.

4

Mattifying SPF 30+

A matte-finish sunscreen controls midday shine while providing broad-spectrum UV protection. The essential final morning step.

🌙 Evening

Deep-cleanse, treat congested pores, reset overnight.

1

Micellar Water or Cleansing Oil

First cleanse to dissolve sunscreen, makeup, and surface pollution without disrupting the barrier. Wipe away gently.

2

Foaming Cleanser (Double Cleanse)

Second cleanse removes residue the first couldn't reach. Critical after wearing sunscreen or any makeup.

3

BHA Exfoliant 2–3× /week

Salicylic acid penetrates pores and dissolves congestion. Use on alternating nights — never the same night as retinol.

4

Lightweight Gel Moisturiser

Even oily skin needs nighttime hydration to support repair. A gel texture won't feel heavy or cause breakouts overnight.

🌅 Morning

Layer hydration and seal it in with rich, nourishing formulas.

1

Creamy or Milky Cleanser

Cleans without stripping the little natural oil dry skin has. Avoid foaming formulas that leave skin feeling uncomfortably tight.

2

Hydrating Toner or Essence

Layer lightweight moisture before heavier products. Pat in gently — never wipe — to avoid further irritation.

3

Hyaluronic Acid Serum

Apply to damp skin and follow immediately with moisturiser to seal the hydration in. Skipping the moisturiser negates this step for dry skin.

4

Rich Cream Moisturiser

Look for ceramides, shea butter, or squalane. Cream formulas seal in previous layers and provide long-lasting comfort throughout the day.

5

Hydrating SPF 50

Choose a dewy or moisturising sunscreen formula — not matte, which can highlight dry patches and cause flaking.

🌙 Evening

Deep nourishment and overnight barrier repair.

1

Gentle Oil-Based Cleanser

Melts away sunscreen and makeup while nourishing dry skin rather than stripping it. Far less drying than foaming cleansers for this type.

2

Hydrating Toner

Glycerin or HA-rich toner patted in while skin is still slightly damp from cleansing maximises hydration absorption.

3

Ceramide or Peptide Serum

Rebuild and repair the barrier as you sleep. Peptides stimulate collagen; ceramides replenish the protective lipid layer.

4

Rich Night Cream or Sleeping Mask

Seal everything in with a thick nourishing cream. Use an intensive sleeping mask 1–2× per week for a major hydration treatment.

🌅 Morning

Balance is the goal — hydrate without overloading oily zones.

1

Balancing Gel Cleanser

Neither too stripping nor too rich — a balanced formula cleanses all zones without triggering either oiliness or tightness.

2

Niacinamide Toner

Addresses oiliness, pores, redness, and uneven tone simultaneously — niacinamide's versatility makes it ideal for combination skin's multiple needs.

3

Medium-Weight Lotion Moisturiser

Hydrates drier cheeks without overloading the T-zone. Avoid both heavy creams and ultra-light gels — a lightweight lotion is the sweet spot.

4

Balanced SPF 30+

A fluid or lotion sunscreen formula works best — neither aggressively mattifying nor overly dewy.

🌙 Evening

Customise by zone — richer on dry areas, lighter on oily.

1

Double Cleanse

Micellar water followed by a gentle gel cleanser ensures thorough removal from all skin zones. Essential after wearing SPF or makeup.

2

Niacinamide Serum (All Over)

Balances sebum, minimises pores, fades uneven tone, and calms redness — addressing all of combination skin's main concerns in one product.

3

Zone-Targeted Moisturiser

Richer cream on cheeks, lighter gel on the T-zone. This personalised approach gives each zone exactly what it needs — nothing more, nothing less.

🌅 Morning

Fewer ingredients, gentler formulas, fragrance-free everything.

1

Fragrance-Free Cream Cleanser

Foaming cleansers frequently trigger sensitive skin. A fragrance-free cream or gentle micellar water is far less likely to cause a reaction.

2

Calming Toner or Essence

Look for centella asiatica, aloe vera, allantoin, or panthenol — all clinically proven to reduce redness and calm inflammation effectively.

3

Simple Ceramide Moisturiser

Fewer ingredients means lower reaction risk. Fragrance-free, ceramide-rich formulas are the gold standard for sensitive and reactive skin.

4

Mineral SPF 30+ (Zinc Oxide)

Physical sunscreens sit on the skin surface rather than absorbing into it, making them far less irritating than chemical UV filters for sensitive types.

🌙 Evening

Minimal steps, maximum gentleness — let skin recover overnight.

1

Micellar Water (No-Rinse)

A gentle micellar water is often all sensitive skin needs at night. No harsh rubbing, no surfactant-heavy formulas, no unnecessary friction.

2

Calming Serum

Centella asiatica or panthenol (Vitamin B5) actively support overnight skin healing and help reduce redness from the day's environmental exposure.

3

Fragrance-Free Night Moisturiser

Keep the ingredient list short and simple. Always patch test any new product on your inner arm before applying to your full face.
